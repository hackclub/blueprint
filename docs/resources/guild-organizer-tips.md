# Guild Organizer Tips

> This page is a work in progress. If you have any suggestions, DM @elliottp on Slack.

## General Tips

- We will help and support you in any way we can, but it is ultimately up to you to make the event happen
- **#build-guilds-organizers** is a good place to ask any questions, especially things other organizers might like to know or could help you with. If something can't be discussed publicly, DM @elliottp
- Use your guild's channel frequently to communicate with attendees.
- This is intended to be a very high agency event. You have complete freedom to decide what you want your guild to look like. You could have a structured day packed with activities or just hang out and show each other your projects. See what people are interested in!
- Start working on your venue and promotion as early as possible
- Figure out when most people are free, if you're not sure try to organize for Saturday or Sunday
- Assume around **50% of your signups will actually show up**

## Budgeting

- Assume you will get **50% attendance**
- You'll get **$8 per signup** for food, materials, venue, etc. from Hack Club
- It can be a good idea to break everything down by cost per attendee to figure out what is realistic
- Prioritize spending on things that will actually be used e.g. food and workshop materials
- Try to make your activities as cost effective as possible e.g. borrowing tools
- Ways to get more funding:
  - Apply for additional funding (we will have a form soon where you can send us a detailed budget and explain what you'd like more funding for)
  - Find local sponsors (see the sponsorship section below)

## Finding a Venue

Finding a venue early is really important. It can be good to pretend you have a week less than you actually do to find one.

**Examples of good venues:**
- Makerspaces
- Libraries
- Company offices
- Universities
- Schools

**What you need at a venue:**
- Power outlets
- Tables
- Wi-Fi
- Some open space if you have more than 10 people
- Ideally the venue should be free

**Tips for finding venues:**
- You might need to reach out to **10+ venues** to get one to say yes, don't get discouraged
- Explain what your event is. Use photos from past Hack Club events to make it feel real
- Do a bit of research into the company/organization and tailor each email to them, but keep them **concise and to the point**. Do not use AI to write the email. Let your excitement about your event shine through
- Try to find and email specific people within the organization. LinkedIn can be a good resource for this
- Try to find free venues, but if they want to charge you, ask if they have a community/non-profit rate. Mention that The Hack Foundation is a 501(c)(3) non-profit
- Use any connections you might have e.g. someone's parent might work at a company that's willing to donate space for your guild
- Be good ambassadors for Hack Club. Don't be rude or spam if they don't respond
- It can be helpful to keep track of where you've emailed in a spreadsheet

## Promotion and Outreach

- Parents group chats
- Email teachers
- Any connections you might have to local coding organizations
- Instagram and other social media
- Physical posters around your school (with permission)
- Use the `/guild-invite` command in your guild's Slack channel to generate an invite link you can share

## On the Day

- **Timeblock your activities.** If you are planning to run specific workshops and activities **timeblock them** so you have a rough schedule so the day has structure and you get through everything you have planned
- If anyone ships new projects, encourage them to put them on GitHub. It would be great to create a Build Guilds compilation after the events
- **Take lots of photos!**
